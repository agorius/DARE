# White Paper: Architectural Exploration and Engineering Analysis
## Optimizing 3 MW Thermal Accumulation and Rejection Loops for Low-Pressure Infrastructure

**Author:** Distributed Engineering Group  
**Date:** May 2026  

---

## 1. Executive Summary
Managing a continuous **3 MW thermal output** over a **12-hour accumulation cycle** yields a massive total energy storage requirement of **36 MWh (thermal)** or **129.6 GJ**. At a source temperature of 120°C, the system architecture dictates a strict choice between structural complexity (high-pressure containment) and spatial/mechanical footprint scaling (atmospheric, unpressurized handling). 

This paper outlines the architectural trade-offs explored across varying materials and pressure boundaries, concluding with the optimal design path: dropping the operational fluid below 100°C to eliminate high-pressure ASME infrastructure, shifting the thermal burden entirely onto fluid dynamics and optimized air-side surface area.
